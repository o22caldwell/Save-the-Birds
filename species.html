Species Affected

  • Kiwi

    The iconic Kiwi is perhaps New Zealand's most famous bird. kiwi are known for their tiny wings and inability to fly. Kiwis are unique because they are the only bird in the world with nostrils at the end of its beak. Unfortunately, only 5% of Kiwi chicks survive in the wild.

  • Tui

    The name tui comes from the Maori language. When European settlers first arrived in New Zealand, they called the Tui a parson bird or mockingbird. Male tuis are identifiable by a small white tuff under their chin.

  • Kakapo

    Kakapo are the worlds only flightless parrot. Historicaly, Kakapo were kept as pets by Maori and early European settlers. These friendly birds are critically endangered and there are only around 300 left in the wild.
